Rules

Season Setup

  1. Each season will be 12 matches long, with four teams competing.
  2. Each match will be 9 holes.
  3. First place team receives 3 points, second 2 points, third 1 point, and fourth 0 points. In the case of tie, ranks will be awarded via Standard Competition Ranking, or in short, "1 + the number of competitors ranked above it."
  4. At the end of the season, there will be a single game playoff with seeding based on points earned.
  5. Each team can have two discs, a primary driver and a primary putter. There is no restriction on their uses, in match.
  6. Ties are allowed in season, but during the playoffs, ties will be settled by playing sudden death holes until a winner emerges.
  7. In the case of a season point tie in the playoffs, the tie will be settled by a play in game, using playoff match rules.

Match Rules

  1. Any disc flying more than 6 feet out of bounds shall be issued a penalty stroke in addition to playing from where it lies.
  2. Everything else is fairly standard.

News

September 3rd, 2020

Statement on the loss of Boss and Shark

It is with great sadness that the league announces the loss of both Shark and Boss, legendary golfers who, in separate, non-league games, were lost to the Wilderness in August.

On August 10th at Burnet Woods, Shark was executing a routine approach shot on hole two when a fluke error sent Shark flying into the Wilderness. No one heard Shark land, and after a long search, Shark was declared lost.

On exiting the Wilderness after the search, a disc was recovered from the Wilderness, another Innova Shark, which we now know as Shark Jr. Theologians and conspiracy theorists alike are still debating the significance of this event.

Later in the week, Boss, obviously distressed from the loss of Shark, on another routine throw, went low and shallow into the tall grass on hole 16 at Heritage Park.. Although seemingly an easy recovery, Boss' yellow color sealed their fate, and after a long search, Boss was declared as Lost.

Boss and Shark were the foundational team of the BDGL and the most experienced veterans. Because of their tragic and losses, the BDGL has implemented a new rule to encourage less aggressive play: any disc flying more than 6 feet into an out of bounds area will be issued a penalty stroke and have to play from where it lies.

In this time of grieving and reflection, we hope to honor the lives of our friends by continuing play. In this spirit, a new team, the Flamingo Flyers, have been called up from the minors to take their place.

About

Brian's Disc Golf League (BDGL) is a prestigious disc golf league where discs compete for the coveted Blue Boss Memorial Disc, our championship trophy that is currently in the Wilderness somewhere.
