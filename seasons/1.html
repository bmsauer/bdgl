Recap

Game note: Matches started on hole 3.

St. Anthony's Army: In a shocker to start the season, St. Anthony's Army pulled out the surprise victory in the season opener. Vanish was able to squash the competition with consistent and accurate drives, while Saint, in its new role as a putter, provided serviceable short game, using its weight against the wind. A missed put on the tough hole 4, and a drive into a tree on hole 8 were the only obstacles in an otherwise par day. It remains to be seen how this team will perform in higher stakes scenarios on more difficult courses.

Frequent Flyers: Tying for second place, the Frequent Flyers put on a disappointing showing for being the league favorites. Overall, Boss had powerful and accurate drives, but Shark couldn't seem to seal the deal for the birdie. A disasterous hole 1 sealed their fate, due to a treed drive and a dismal second shot from Shark. Shark did, however, prove useful, teeing off on holes 8 and 9 with well placed drives, and finishing up with the approach and putt.

Misfits: Another surprise today coming from Misfits, delivering a commendable performance and a tie for second against the league favorite. Kicked off with surprisingly strong drives for Slice, they quickly degraded into tree magnets. Things went further downhill when an approach shot from Soul Platter went way off course, sealing the deal on a bogie. This rattled the team into hole 7, and Slice took over many of the approach duties afterwards. Soul Platter was able to partially redeem itself with a great approach shot on 1, which gave ther teams fits. Misfits were the only team to record par on hole 4, one of the most difficult holes at Embshoff, besides 1.

Pioneers: Truly disasterous day from the Pioneers to which the score doesn't reflect. The Pioneers consider themselves lucky to be only 1 stroke behind the pack, as Mitch's drives went into other fairways, the woods, or 30ft ahead of the tee. Aviar didn't make a single shot it needed to make, and if it wasn't for some heroic approach shots from Mitch, this team would have been truly sunk. Pioneers will need a lot of practice if they intend to compete on longer, more difficult courses.
